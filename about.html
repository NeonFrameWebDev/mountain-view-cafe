Our Story.

A Green Valley lunch cafe, family-owned

The Short Version

Twelve years of feeding Green Valley.

Nancy Byrd and her late husband opened Mountain View Cafe and Bakery in 2014, tucked into a small space off Duval Road with a view of the Santa Rita Mountains out the window. The idea was plain. Make the sandwiches from scratch. Bake the bread and the cakes and the tarts in-house. Treat every regular like a regular.

Twelve years later we are still here, still scratch-cooking, and still family-owned. The pandemic did not close us. Time has not changed us. We just keep putting lunch on the counter.

“If you can get a good sandwich and people love it, we keep it.”
Nancy Byrd, Owner

What Makes Us, Us

A few things we refuse to compromise on.

01

Everything from Scratch

Bread baked in-house. Soups simmered each morning. Dressings, chicken salad, prickly pear BBQ, cheesecake filling. All made here.

02

Southwestern Roots

Prickly pear iced tea, prickly pear BBQ meatloaf, Southwestern turkey melt, green chile when we can get it good. Sonoran through and through.

03

A Small Place, On Purpose

We are never going to be big. We know our regulars by name, their orders by memory, and their kids when they visit from out of town.

04

Nancy in the Bakery

Every cake, every tart, every cheesecake. Nancy is in the back before the door opens. The case fills up, the case empties, she bakes more tomorrow.

05

Green Valley, Year-Round

We serve the snowbirds in winter, the retirees all year, the Tucson day-trippers on weekends, and the Sahuarita lunch crowd every Friday.

06

Twelve Years In

Featured on KGUN9 at our 12-year anniversary. Still the same menu philosophy. Still the same owner. Still the same door.
